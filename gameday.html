The Journey

From the first pitch to the final out

The Dominican Republic entered the 2026 WBC with the most anticipated roster the tournament had ever seen. Pundits called them "unbeatable." Fans called them "destiny." And for six games, they lived up to every expectation.

The Pool Play stage was a statement. A 10-2 demolition of Cuba, an 8-inning masterpiece from Sandy Alcántara against Venezuela, and an electrifying performance from Juan Soto against Puerto Rico. The Super Round brought a tight win over the Netherlands and a gut-punch defeat to Japan that proved the road to the championship would be hard.

Then came the Semifinal against the United States. Trailing in the 9th, Juan Soto stepped into the batter's box. What followed — a three-run walkoff homer — became the defining image of Dominican baseball in 2026. The nation stopped. The streets erupted.

The Final against Japan was an epic worthy of the occasion. A lead lost, a furious comeback, and a heartbreaking last out. But the 2026 Dominican Republic WBC team will forever be remembered not for falling short, but for how they made everyone believe — including themselves.
